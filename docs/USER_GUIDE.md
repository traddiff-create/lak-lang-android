# User Guide — LakLang

## Getting Started

LakLang is a Lakota language learning app. Open it and start exploring — no account needed, no internet required.

## Spotlight Search

Tap the magnifying glass icon at the top of any screen. A search overlay appears. Type any word in Lakota or English. Results appear instantly, grouped by type:

- **Words** — Lakota vocabulary with translations
- **Stories** — Ella Deloria narratives
- **Culture** — Articles about Lakota worldview and language
- **Values** — Cultural values and concepts
- **People** — Historical figures
- **Dialogues** — Conversational examples

Tap any result to go to its detail page. Tap X or press Back to close search.

## Explore Tab

Browse the full vocabulary by category. Swipe the category chips (All, Abstract, Actions, Animals, Body, Colors...) to filter. The Word of the Day card shows a random Lakota word each time you open the app.

Tap any word to see its full detail page.

## Word Detail

The heart of LakLang. For every word you see:

- **Lakota word** in LLC Standard Orthography
- **Phonetic guide** — simplified pronunciation
- **IPA** — International Phonetic Alphabet transcription
- **English meaning**
- **Cultural Context** — what this word means in Lakota life
- **Related Words** — other words in the same category
- **Connected Stories** — narratives that reference this concept
- **Connected Values** — cultural values this word expresses
- **Pronunciation Breakdown** — each character explained with its sound

Tap the bookmark icon to save the word for later.

## Stories Tab

63 stories from Ella Deloria's *Dakota Texts* (1932). Each story shows:

- Lakota and English titles
- The full narrative text
- **"This story teaches:"** — connected cultural values
- **"People in this story:"** — historical figures and characters

## Learn Tab

Three sections:

### Pronunciation
The LLC Standard Lakota Orthography — every vowel, consonant, and diacritic with its IPA equivalent, English approximation, and example word.

### Culture
24 articles on Lakota worldview, language structure, social organization, and traditional practices.

### Dialogues
19 conversational examples with Lakota text, English translation, and speaker exchanges.

## Saved Tab

All your bookmarked words, stories, and articles in one place. Tap any saved item to return to its detail page.

## Cosmos Tab — Seven Sacred Directions

The Cosmos is a living museum of Lakota knowledge. A dark starfield shows 12,535+ items orbiting across seven sacred directions. Pinch to zoom, drag to pan.

### The Seven Directions

- **Wiyohpeyata (West)** — Stories. Place of the Thunder Beings and introspection.
- **Waziyata (North)** — Wisdom. Place of the White Giant, where cold brings clarity.
- **Wihinanpata (East)** — Knowledge. Place of the rising sun and new beginnings.
- **Itokaga (South)** — Community. Place of warmth and growth.
- **Wankantanhan (Sky)** — Sacred. The realm above, home of Wakȟáŋ Tȟáŋka.
- **Maka (Earth)** — Land. Grandmother Earth and all living things below.
- **Cante (Center)** — Heart. Where all directions meet — your personal learning center.

**Tap a direction** to enter its immersive page with a cosmological description and all related content. Tap any item to see its full detail page.

**Tap any orbiting node** to see its Lakota name, English meaning, and graph connections to other items.

The current season's direction glows brighter — spring highlights East (vocabulary/knowledge).

## Tips

- Search works in both Lakota and English
- The pronunciation breakdown on word detail pages helps you sound out unfamiliar words
- Connected Stories and Values show how words live in Lakota culture, not just what they translate to
- The Cosmos tab shows how all knowledge is connected — zoom in to see individual word labels
- Each direction has its own color — follow the colors to understand the thematic groupings
