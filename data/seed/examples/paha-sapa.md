Paha Sapa, the Black Hills, are the sacred center of the Lakota world. The name comes from the dark appearance of the pine-covered hills when seen from the plains.

The Black Hills are central to Lakota identity and spirituality. They are the place of creation stories, vision quests, and ceremonies that have been practiced for generations.
