# Privacy Policy — LakLang

**Effective Date:** April 3, 2026
**Developer:** Traditionally Different Technology LLC
**Contact:** traddiff@gmail.com

## Summary

LakLang does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data. The app works entirely offline.

## Data Collection

LakLang collects **no data whatsoever**:

- No personal information (name, email, phone, location)
- No usage analytics or telemetry
- No crash reports
- No advertising identifiers
- No device information
- No cookies or tracking pixels

## Data Storage

The app stores two types of data, both locally on your device only:

1. **Educational content** — A pre-built database of Lakota vocabulary, stories, and cultural materials bundled with the app
2. **Your bookmarks** — Words and stories you choose to save, stored in the app's local database

This data never leaves your device. There is no cloud sync, no backup service, and no server communication.

## Network Access

LakLang makes **zero network requests**. The app requires no internet connection to function. No data is transmitted to any server, third party, or external service.

## Permissions

LakLang requests **no device permissions**. No access to camera, microphone, contacts, location, storage, or any other device capability.

## Third-Party Services

LakLang uses **no third-party services** — no analytics (Firebase, Amplitude), no advertising (AdMob), no crash reporting (Crashlytics), no attribution tracking.

## Children's Privacy

LakLang is suitable for all ages. Since no data is collected, there are no concerns regarding children's privacy under COPPA or similar regulations.

## Cultural Data

The Lakota language content in this app is treated with respect for cultural data sovereignty. Content is sourced from public domain and openly licensed academic materials. It is not used to train AI models and is not shared with third parties.

## Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be included with the app update. Since no data is collected, there is little reason for this policy to change.
