## Full Description (4,000 chars max)

LakLang is a Lakota language learning app built for people with real ties to the language and community. Every word is a doorway back into the worldview.

SEARCH EVERYTHING
Type any word — in Lakota or English — and instantly find vocabulary, stories, cultural articles, values, historical figures, and dialogues. One search, all knowledge connected.

DEEP WORD EXPLORATION
Tap any word and discover its full depth: meaning, pronunciation breakdown, cultural context, related words, connected stories, and the values it expresses. This isn't a flashcard — it's understanding.

63 LAKOTA STORIES
Read narratives from Ella Deloria's Dakota Texts (1932), with connected cultural values and historical figures. See what each story teaches and who appears in it.

LEARN THE SOUNDS
42 pronunciation guides covering every vowel, consonant, and diacritic in Lakota Language Consortium Standard Orthography. Each with IPA transcription and English approximation.

CULTURAL UNDERSTANDING
24 articles on Lakota worldview, language structure, social organization, and traditional practices. Learn why the language works the way it does.

PRACTICE CONVERSATIONS
19 dialogue examples with Lakota text, English translation, and speaker exchanges for conversational practice.

SAVE & STUDY
Bookmark any word, story, or article. Build your personal study collection.

COMPLETELY OFFLINE
No internet required. No ads. No tracking. No data collection. Your learning is private.

CONTENT INTEGRITY
All content is sourced from verified academic and community materials. Lakota Language Consortium Standard Orthography. No AI-generated Lakota. Respectful presentation.

Content sources include Ella Deloria's Dakota Texts (public domain), Comparative Siouan Dictionary (CC-BY 4.0), Digital Lakota Corpus (Apache 2.0), and Lakota Language Consortium reference materials.
