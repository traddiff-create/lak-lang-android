Hi there,

My name is Rory Stone. I run a small technology company called Traditionally Different Technology here in Rapid City. I'm reaching out because we've been building something for the Lakota language that I think your team would find interesting — and I'd love to get on a call to show you.

Over the past few weeks we've built an open-source knowledge graph for Lakota language and cultural preservation. It's a web platform where words, stories, values, people, places, ceremonies, and songs are all interconnected — so a learner can see that a word appears in a story, that story teaches a value, that value is expressed through a ceremony. Everything linked, the way the language actually works.

We've ingested content from openly licensed sources — the Comparative Siouan Dictionary (688 words), Ella Deloria's Dakota Texts (62 stories), and the Digital Lakota Corpus. We use LLC Standard Lakota Orthography throughout and your free resources have been foundational for our pronunciation guides and seed vocabulary.

What we don't have — and what nobody else has — is the depth of the New Lakota Dictionary. The knowledge graph needs that connective tissue. We're not looking to copy or compete with NLD. We're looking to explore whether there's a way our platforms could complement each other — whether that's an API integration, deep linking, or something we haven't thought of yet.

A few things that might matter:

- Everything we build is open source and free
- All content goes through a community review pipeline before reaching learners — nothing publishes without approval
- Our AI tutor (Claude) is explicitly prohibited from generating Lakota — it only uses verified vocabulary as context
- We're in Rapid City. We're not going anywhere.

I'd love 20-30 minutes on a call or video chat to show you the platform and hear your thoughts. We're builders, not linguists — your team knows what the language needs better than we do, and we'd rather build the right thing together than guess alone.

Would anyone on your team be open to a conversation?
